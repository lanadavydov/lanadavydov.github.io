We have been taught to see and understand art, images, and the world around us. Our perceptions are shaped by the historical and social contexts in which we live, and that the meaning and value of art are not fixed or objective, but are shaped by our own experiences and perspectives.

We must question the assumptions that underpin our understanding of art and images, and to recognize the political and ideological dimensions of visual culture.

We should embrace a more critical and self-reflexive approach to art, one that recognizes the complexity and diversity of human experience.

This song reflects Berger's critique of the Western tradition of art as a means of representing the world in a "transparent" or "realistic" manner. The lyrics suggest that there is more to reality than what is visible on the surface. What we deem a the "truth" is a vision into our own perceptions and biases.

Our ways of seeing are not natural nor inevitable. Rather, they are shaped by the historical and social contexts in which we live. By challenging our assumptions and questioning the images surrounding us, we can develop a more critical and self-aware understanding of visual culture, and of the world around us.
