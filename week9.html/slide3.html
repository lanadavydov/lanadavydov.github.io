Constant Dullaart is a Dutch conceptual artist known for his works that examine the influence of the internet on contemporary society.

Dullaart's mission as an artist is to explore the impact of digital media and the internet on our lives, and how this affects the way we perceive reality, truth, and power. He is particularly interested in the ways in which online platforms shape our understanding of information and communication, and how this affects our social and political systems.

Dullaart started incorporating the internet into his art in the early 2000s, and was drawn to the internet as a medium because of its unique properties (such as its ability to connect people across vast distances, its capacity for mass dissemination of information, and its potential for decentralized power structures).

Dullaart's early works often involved hacking or manipulating existing online platforms or technologies, such as Google, Facebook, and Twitter, in order to subvert their intended functions or draw attention to their flaws. By subverting the conventions of web design, Dullaart aimed to draw attention to the limitations and biases of online communication.
